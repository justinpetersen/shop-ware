Closed Loop

Edit This
shopware | August 5, 2014

This is a blog about Shop-Ware: why it works, how it works, and what people think about it. This is the first post, and future posts will record where we go from here.

“Closed Loop” refers to modern engine control: adapting and maximizing performance based on real world results. It relies on feedback: a sensor reports exhaust chemistry to a computer, which adjusts fuel control in response. The “closed loop” connects results with the recipe.

The same concept exists in software: a minimum viable product, agile development, and validated learning. Over are the days of dictating what people want. We predict what you want; test it; and respond accordingly. We judge our product based on your enthusiasm, not our own.

There is no sales pitch, there is no convincing. Either you want it or you don’t. We adapt accordingly. Our mission statement: Shop-Ware works for you.
